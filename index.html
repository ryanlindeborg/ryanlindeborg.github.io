Research

  1. Anticipating Unintended Robot Behaviors with Side Effect Critics
    Ryan Lindeborg*, Richard Jiankun Peng*, Ayush Jain, Jesse Zhang, Abrar Anwar, and Jesse Thomason
    Spotlight Presentation, Rethinking Safety Workshop, RSS 2026
    We study side effect prediction for robotic manipulation, aiming to anticipate unintended changes before they occur, so the robot can stop, resample, or defer to a human. We train a side effect critic, initialized from a pre-trained vision-language action (VLA) model, that predicts whether given action chunks will cause a side effect before the actions are executed.

  2. TiPToP: A Modular Open-Vocabulary Robot Manipulation System That Plans
    William Shen*, Nishanth Kumar*, Sahit Chintalapudi, Ryan Lindeborg, Jie Wang, Christopher Watson, Edward S. Hu, Jing Cao, Dinesh Jayaraman, Leslie Pack Kaelbling, and Tomás Lozano-Pérez
    FM4RoboPlan Workshop, RSS 2026
